KNOWLEDGE NUGGET: 📈 Why 1 in 10^467 for the Flagellum?

This huge number isn’t random. It comes from stacking the odds for each core flagellum module.

  • Biophysics estimates that getting a single, smallish, functional protein fold (150 Amino Acids) by chance can be on the order of 1 in 10^77 (See Douglas Axe reasearch).
  • The flagellum nano motor needs many different proteins working together (for stator, rotor, drive shaft, hook, filament, export). The motor is made up of 30 different protein parts.
  • The flagellum nano motor is made up of approximately 30 different protein parts.
  • The flagellum motor measures approximately 25 to 50 nanometeres in diameter, a human hair average diameter is 100 micrometers. This means 4,000,000 of these motors could fit in the cross sectional area of a human hair.
  • When you combine multiple such modules, the total probability collapses exponentially, giving exponents in the hundreds.

So a result like 1 in 10467 is a conservative illustration of how fast the odds crash once you require multiple coordinated, correctly folded, correctly placed protein systems to appear and work together. This shows an engineering like signature, certainly not an accident.
